# Datasheet for VidTide M0

Following Gebru et al., *Datasheets for Datasets* (CACM 2021), and the NeurIPS 2026 Datasets & Benchmarks track guidelines.

> **Skeleton notice.** This document is the submission-time skeleton; section bodies are stubbed and will be expanded in lock-step with the staged code release in this anonymous repository. The numbers cited below match the paper's Table~`tab:m0_stats` and Appendix~`sec:appendix_m0_dataflow`.

---

## 1. Motivation

- **Purpose.** Provide a *living* in-the-wild benchmark for AI-generated video detection that tracks the rapidly shifting generator frontier and platform redistribution effects, rather than a frozen lab snapshot.
- **Funding & creation.** Withheld for double-blind submission.

## 2. Composition

- **Instances.** Each instance is a single video clip, identified by `(source_platform, source_id)`, accompanied by a `metadata.jsonl` record (see schema below).
- **Total instances (M0 frozen slice).** 22,869 clips across YouTube, Bilibili, Reddit, and official model showcase galleries.
- **Per-instance fields.** `id`, `source_platform`, `source_url`, `source_id`, `label` (`real`/`fake`), `claimed_generator`, `tier_source` (T1/T2/T3), `duration_sec`, `resolution_w`, `resolution_h`, `fps`, `file_size_bytes`, `sha256` (computed at crawl time, stored for integrity checks at re-download time), `title`, `content_tags`, `published_at`, `crawled_at`.
- **Labels.** Three-tier provenance taxonomy:
  - **T1** — official generator-showcase galleries (Pika, Kling, Runway, ...). Treated as 100% AI-generated.
  - **T2** — platform AI-disclosure tags (YouTube AI-disclosure since 2024, Bilibili `argue_info` tag enforcing the China AI labelling regulation since Sept 2025, TikTok C2PA).
  - **T3** — keyword match in title/description with LLM-assisted semantic verification.
- **Splits.** 5K real / 5K fake gap-evaluation slice (paper Table~`tab:gap`); 7,957 / 1,999 fine-tuning train / test split (paper Table~`tab:finetune`).

## 3. Collection process

- Continuous crawl by per-platform harvesters (`crawlers/`).
- Quality, tag-tier, and LLM filters (`filters/`).
- CLIP-based cross-platform deduplication (`dedup/`).
- Cleanlab-based label-quality audit; reported $\kappa = 0.93$ in the paper.
- Pipeline operating cost: ~\$156/month at standard mode.

## 4. Preprocessing

The published `metadata.jsonl` is the post-filter, post-dedup record. Reviewers/users recover the actual video bytes from `source_url` via `scripts/download_videos.py`, which validates the `sha256` field where possible and records any mismatches (e.g. platform re-encoding, URL rot).

## 5. Uses

- **Intended uses.** AI-generated video detection benchmarking; evaluation of robustness to generator drift, platform re-encoding, and provenance-tier mixing.
- **Out-of-scope uses.** Re-identification of uploaders; commercial training of generative models; any use that conflicts with platform Terms of Service.

## 6. Distribution

- **Format.** Metadata-only release (`metadata.jsonl` + split files). **No video files are redistributed**; reviewers/users fetch clips from the original platform URLs at evaluation time.
- **Hosting.** Anonymous mirror of this repository for double-blind review; HuggingFace `datasets` release after acceptance, following the VidProM CC-BY-NC 4.0 precedent.
- **Per-clip rights.** Original platform Terms of Service apply; per-clip rights remain with the uploader.

## 7. Maintenance

- **Living updates.** Monthly immutable slices (M0, M1, ...). M0 is the frozen submission slice; subsequent monthly slices will be appended without modifying frozen ones.
- **Opt-out.** A 24-hour opt-out flow is described in `docs/OPT_OUT.md`. During the double-blind window, opt-out requests are accepted via the anonymous repository's issue tracker; manifest deletion immediately revokes the clip from all reproduced slices.
- **Errata.** Tracked via the same issue tracker; corrections are recorded as additive errata entries, never as silent rewrites of frozen slices.

## 8. Legal & ethical considerations

- All sources are public, and all `fake`-labelled clips are videos that were *self-disclosed* as AI-generated by their uploaders or originated from official generator galleries; no covertly-classified material is included.
- Faces and personally identifiable content are not augmented; the dataset is for detection-pipeline benchmarking, not for biometric study.
- See paper Section `sec:limitations` and Appendix `sec:datasheet` for the full discussion.
